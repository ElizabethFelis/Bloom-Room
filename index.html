privacy notice

before entering bloom room, please read and accept our privacy policy.

1. introduction

welcome to bloom room. bloom room is a small interactive reflection experience designed to help users select thoughts, arrange simple words, and explore a calm digital environment. this policy explains what the room may observe while you are inside.

2. agreement to enter

by selecting “i agree,” you confirm that you understand this experience may respond to your clicks, typed words, pauses, repeated choices, and movement around the page. if you do not agree, you may close the page before entering.

3. information we do not request

bloom room does not ask for your legal name, home address, passwords, school records, private messages, camera access, microphone access, or financial information. you should not enter personal information into the reflection box.

4. information you choose to provide

the room may process the word you type into the reflection box. this word is used only to shape the experience during the current session. please enter a simple word, not a name, password, address, or other identifying detail.

5. interaction data

to make the room feel responsive, bloom room may observe which buttons you click, which choices you ignore, which areas you hover over, and which objects you return to after being redirected.

6. hesitation and timing

the room may notice hesitation, including pauses before clicking, stopping during typing, returning to an option after avoiding it, or leaving the input box before finishing a word. hesitation is treated as part of the room’s atmosphere.

7. almost-clicked content

some areas of the room may respond even if you do not fully click them. hovering near closed objects, moving toward hidden areas, or repeatedly approaching the same corner may be interpreted as interest.

8. behavioral patterns

bloom room may create a temporary pattern based on your actions. this pattern may include repeated choices, avoided choices, closed areas reopened, and directions your cursor moved before changing its path.

9. identity not required

the room does not need to know your name to respond to you. repeated behavior may be enough to form a temporary impression of what you are likely to do next.

10. guide responses

elizabeth felis, also known as eliza, may respond differently depending on your actions. her facial expression, dialogue, and tone may change if you follow the path, hesitate, or attempt to open areas labeled as unfinished.

11. closed areas

some areas of the room are labeled as closed, unfinished, locked, or not ready. these labels are part of the experience. repeated attempts to open these areas may cause the room to reveal additional content.

12. emotional safety

bloom room contains psychological horror elements, including changes in tone, red flashing effects, distorted text, and unsettling dialogue. users who are uncomfortable may exit by closing the page.

13. visual effects

the experience may use screen flashes, color shifts, text glitches, image changes, and shaking effects. these effects are fictional and are used to support the story.

14. temporary memory

the room may appear to remember your choices during the current session. this includes the word you typed, the buttons you chose, the places you hovered over, and the areas you tried to open.

15. session only

all remembered information is temporary and exists only while the page is open. refreshing the page begins the experience again. the room may still say it remembers. that is part of the story.

16. no real account tracking

this project does not connect to real accounts, databases, servers, cookies, analytics platforms, or external tracking tools. it is a local website demo made with html, css, and javascript.

17. simulated profiling

the room may display a fictional profile or archive based on your actions. this archive is generated from in-page interactions and is not a real data record.

18. pattern prediction

if you repeatedly move toward the same object, the room may behave as though it expected you to return. this is not magic. it is simply the page remembering the path you kept choosing.

19. user responsibility

you are responsible for the choices you make inside the room. if you open something after being warned, the room may treat that as permission to continue.

20. consent through action

some parts of the experience are activated not by words, but by behavior. clicking, hovering, returning, pausing, and refusing to leave may all be treated as forms of interaction.

21. data minimization

bloom room collects as little as possible. sometimes almost nothing is enough.

22. storage of reflections

your typed word is not saved outside the page. however, during the session, the room may use it as part of the final reveal. the room is very good at keeping small things where you left them.

23. emotional interpretation

the room may make fictional guesses about your curiosity, trust, caution, or disobedience. these guesses are part of the horror narrative and should not be interpreted as real psychological analysis.

24. interface changes

the page may become darker, redder, or less friendly as the experience continues. this does not mean anything is wrong with your browser.

25. hidden content

some content is intentionally placed beneath the surface of the first screen. the user may not see this content unless they interact with closed areas or continue after warnings.

26. guide behavior

eliza may begin as helpful, polite, and calm. she may become nervous, serious, or cold if the user repeatedly approaches areas she told them not to open.

27. user choices

the experience may present choices that appear safe, harmless, or decorative. not all choices have equal consequences. some choices are designed to test whether the user trusts the surface.

28. archive reveal

if the archive opens, it may list actions such as repeated choices, closed places reopened, hover patterns, and predicted direction. this is fictional, but it is based on real clicks and hovers inside the page.

29. no sale of information

bloom room does not sell, trade, rent, or transfer user information. the room keeps what it notices inside the room. the room does not need to send it anywhere to make use of it.

30. third parties

this project does not use third-party services. no outside company receives the user’s word, choices, hovers, or session behavior.

31. deletion

to delete the current session, refresh or close the page. if the room appears to remember after that, it is only because the story was written that way.

32. children and students

relex, this is just my Hackathon project. though it should be treated as an interactive story, not a real privacy product or security tool.

33. accuracy of responses

eliza’s statements are not always honest. she may comfort, redirect, warn, or accuse the user depending on their actions. her friendliness should not be understood as proof of safety.

34. surface experience

the visible page may appear soft, quiet, and harmless. this is intentional. the project explores how systems can seem gentle while still observing behavior underneath.

35. beneath the surface

by entering bloom room, you acknowledge that the surface of an interface may not show everything it is doing. some things are only visible after the user has already agreed.

36. final consent

by checking the box below, you confirm that you have had the opportunity to read this policy. whether you actually read every section is not something the room needs to ask. your agreement is enough.
